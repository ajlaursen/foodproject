History

A study done by University of Michigan resulted in findings that show more families are now chronically food insecure than they were 20 years ago. (2) . In 2022, within households with children, 17.3% or 6.4 million households suffered from household food insecurity (2). Specifically in Washtenaw County, the COVID pandemic played a huge factor in food insecure households. In 2020, food pantries saw a reported 30-300% increase in people seeking assistance, with 2.8 million pounds of food being distributed between the months of March 2020 to June 2020 (3).
