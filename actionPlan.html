Action Plan

We are proposing a take home meal plan where children under 18 will be able to pick up a ready to heat meal from the school cafeteria. This will be available Monday through Friday. All children under 18 in Washtenaw county will be eligible for this program regardless of school enrollment. One of the barriers of most free or subsidized meal programs we have noticed is that there is a difficult or confusing process to sign up. We want to make our program available to anyone who is under 18. With this blanket approach we will be able to provide to kids that need it the most. They will not need parental intervention to get this food nor will there be any kind of lengthy sign up process or specific requirements for eligibility besides being under the age of 18. Any kid that walks into a school cafeteria can ask for a meal to be taken home. We believe that this process will allow as many children as possible to participate. We are not worried about kids taking advantage of this program because of the ability to only get one meal per child that walks into the cafeteria. We believe that students in more affluent areas will not take advantage of this program so it will naturally balance out if people are going from school to school to get more than one meal. We will distribute funds to schools on a reimbursement basis with an initial investment of funds to allow schools to start the program.

With roughly 50,000 kids under 18 in Washtenaw county and an estimated cost of $3.34 And an estimated 34,000 kids that would take advantage of the program we are proposing a $0.19 gas tax to cover the cost of this program. With a maximum of a $0.25 gas tax to cover the program if there were a 100% utilization for the program.
